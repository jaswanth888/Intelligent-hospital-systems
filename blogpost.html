Eating to Lift Your Winter Blues

What are Winter Blues?
Many of us feel extremely tired and low around the time winter begins. This feeling of sadness and depression is also called winter blues. Winter blues or Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) affects a large segment of the population. It is depression that occurs every year during a particular season and can affect your mood and well-being. It is a treatable condition, but it is important to notice its triggering factors and make suitable lifestyle changes.
What are the Symptoms of SAD?
The symptoms of SAD are more or less similar to depression. However, they tend to occur at a particular season every year. Some of the symptoms of SAD related to the winter season are: Lethargy. You feel exhausted and lack energy most of the time during the day. Hypersomnia. You feel the need to sleep excessively. Weight Gain. Your appetite is affected as you start overeating and gain weight. Loneliness. Winter blues can also cause you to feel socially isolated and lonely. Decreased Concentration. You may have difficulty focusing on one task and get distracted easily. Hopelessness. You may continuously worry and stop feeling hopeful about the future.
What are the Causes Behind Your Winter Blues?
The specific cause behind this disorder remains unclear. However, various factors that may play a role are: Decreased Hormone Levels. A reduction in various hormones in your body may also cause this disorder. Disturbed Biological Clock. Sunlight is not available abundantly during winters. This may disrupt your internal biological clock and lead to its onset. When to Seek Medical Attention? It is okay to feel down at times, but if you feel lethargic and depressed for days, then you should seek medical attention. If there are changes in appetite or sleep patterns, it may be a cause for concern.

Food Problems: Is It an Allergy or Intolerance?

Eating food is essential, as it is a nutrition source that keeps you healthy and energized. Sadly, you may find yourself getting ill after eating certain types of food. The instant reaction would be to think of allergies, but it may not be so. Your body will reject food items; it is food intolerance. There is no instant reaction when you are intolerant to some food products. You may be able to eat a small amount without any trouble. On the contrary, a food allergy may be more severe and even life threatening. You will find several signs and symptoms associated with it, which allows your doctor to diagnose the condition immediately. How do you differentiate between the two? You need to be aware of each and understand your bodily reactions to determine your status correctly.
Food Allergy: What is it?
An allergic reaction triggered in response to certain food items entering the body is termed as an abnormal immune response, as your body mistakenly assumes that the food is harmful to your body. Food allergy is usually evident the first time that you have a particular type of food. Nuts, shellfish, eggs, peanuts, and milk are everyday items causing food allergies.
Food Intolerance: What is it?
Your digestive system’s abnormal response to certain types of food is called intolerance. Food, where digestive enzymes cannot break it down properly may cause you to feel uneasy and throw up. Milk and milk products often cause intolerance.
